About Me

Hey! My name is Daniel Anorue Jr, a (Junior) Computer Science major at The University of Southern Mississippi.

I aim to specialize in Software Engineering (Full-Stack). I take extracurricular classes in order to bolster my skillset toward becoming a decent Software Developer. I’ve done courses with IBM, FreeCodeCamp, and CodePath to that effect.

Career-wise, I haven’t yet secured an internship, co-op, or part-time job directly related to software engineering, but I’m consistently improving my skills. My closest experience was helping my cousin’s startup build their website, where I learned about collaborative development and core web fundamentals. Although, I have (and still currently am) worked part-time in other industries — as a technician, a fry cook, a caterer, and an events management crew member — in common, these jobs taught me a lot about how to work with customers and as a team member. Most importantly, I learned how important I am as an individual cog in the company machinery, and how my role contributes to keeping operations running smoothly — and how small errors and lapses could lead to the “machine getting faulty.” These lessons and job-specific skills I have gained have helped me navigate not just the job market but life in general. I apply some of these fundamentals to my school life, and they have made me a more responsible person.

As for projects, my biggest motivation is to work on something impactful to other people. I hoped to do that in an internship where there is already an established infrastructure (funding, user base, and all that), but I haven’t been fortunate to get one yet. However, that hasn’t stopped me from embarking on small projects here and there to test my skills, like my social media site ThinkOutLoud and my brewery locator Rum Barrel. This previous summer, I decided to do something on a bigger scale — an application tracker with some social media capabilities (being able to add friends and share opportunities on the site). It’s called InternIn. This is by far my favorite project, and I’ve devoted so much time to it. I try to make updates whenever I get time from school. I actively use it and maybe I might advertise it more when it’s polished.

I try to be involved as much as I can. Having been a member of several notable campus groups like NSBE and Google Developer Groups, I decided to challenge myself by starting my own ColorStack chapter here at Southern Miss. My aim is to build a supportive community for minority tech majors so as to help them feel confident in their journey through college. We are just starting, but I think it has the potential to become a great investment because USM desperately needed something like this.

In my free time, I love playing video games, basketball, going for walks, or watching anime. (Fun fact: a majority of my life skills are from video games!)


I am open to connecting and making friends and possibly collabortaing in projects, but most importantly — I Am Open To Work!
